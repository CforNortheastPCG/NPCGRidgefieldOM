\Northeast Private Client Group - Brand Style Guide
This document provides a comprehensive guide to the brand and editorial style for Northeast Private Client Group. Following these guidelines ensures consistency across all communications and reinforces our brand identity.

Logo
Our logo is our most important visual asset. It should be used consistently and correctly in all applications.

Primary Logo: The official logo is the stacked, two-color version. It should be used whenever possible.

Clear Space: To ensure visibility and impact, maintain a clear space around the logo. The minimum clear space is one-third the height of the logo.

Improper Use: Do not alter the logo in any way. This includes, but is not limited to:

Changing the colors, fonts, or proportions.

Tilting, rotating, or flipping the logo.

Distorting, stretching, or skewing the logo.

Adding drop shadows or other visual effects.

Placing the logo on a busy or visually confusing background.

Brand Voice & Tone
Our voice reflects our role as trusted, expert advisors who are also confident team players.

Voice Characteristics
Expert (Guiding): We provide knowledge and understanding backed by data and research to help clients make smart decisions. Be trustworthy, clear, and encouraging.

Team Player (Confident): We communicate candidly and with confidence to motivate clients. Be straightforward, genuine, and results-driven.

Engineer (Empowering): We use an active voice to help clients realize their potential and achieve their vision. Be goals-focused and use action-oriented language.

Tone of Voice
Our tone should always be:

Serious, not humorous.

Conversational, not formal.

Matter-of-fact, not overly emotional.

Respectful, not flippant.

Brand Language
Incorporate these words and phrases to align with our brand personality:

Expert: Security, Stability, Prosperity, Advisory, Guidance, Sound advice, Smarter decisions.

Team Player: Confidence, Opportunity, Collaboration, Maximize value, Proactive process.

Engineer: Empower, Inspire, Fulfilled, Build wealth, Positive transformation.

Imagery
Photography: Use high-quality, architectural photography of properties. Show interesting angles and details. For lifestyle shots, show realistic scenes of families and professionals; avoid posed, direct-to-camera shots.

Color Treatment: Photos should be bright and inviting. A duotone style using Carbon Blue or Stone Beige can be used for contrast or lighter applications.

Patterns
An architectural, repeating cube pattern can be used as a background element to enhance the brand's visual identity. It can be used in light, dark, small-scale, or large-scale variations.
